2024 Elections: Refugee Board warns asylum seekers against political involvement





The Executive Secretary of the Ghana Refugee Board, Tetteh Padi, has emphasised the importance of keeping refugee asylum seekers away from political activities.

In an interview with Citi News on Friday, Padi highlighted the clear rules that prohibit refugees from engaging in politics, whether in their country of origin or their country of asylum.

Padi stated, “Refugee asylum seekers are sensitized not to get involved in politics. In fact, the rules are very clear: don’t be involved in politics either in your country of origin or that of your country of asylum, and we keep on sensitizing them and we make sure that this is enforced.”

He further explained that while the Ghana Refugee Board provides humanitarian assistance, they are also acutely aware of the security situation in the area.
